Seattle based Companies

Fortune 500 Companies

Costco

Costco is a global chain of membership warehouse stores where customers buy in bulk to receive discounts. The stores offer groceries, appliances, home goods, clothing, and toiletries. Most stores also have snack bars, pharmacies, and optical centers. Beyond in-store services, Costco offers members access to a wide range of home services, insurance, loans and more. As an employer, Costco often ranks as one of the best places to work in Washington State. Benefits for eligible employees include health and dental care, pharmacy and vision center access at deep discounts, dependent care assistance, life insurance, and other work-life benefits. There are locations in many Puget Sound cities, including Seattle, Tacoma, Puyallup, Covington, Issaquah, Everett, and Federal Way.

Microsoft

Microsoft is a software giant, known worldwide for creating some of the best-known software from Office to Skype to one of the most popular operating systems in the world—Windows. There are Microsoft jobs in locations on just about every continent, but the corporate headquarters is just outside of Seattle in Redmond on a large campus. Of course, Microsoft has ample IT jobs—programmers, hardware and software engineers, testers—but you might be surprised by the number of non-technical jobs, too. The corporate campus employs people in its store, in sales and marketing, human resources, and customer service. A special bonus, Microsoft offers opportunities for students and recent graduates, too. Work-life benefits are extensive and include maternity and paternity leave, child care assistance, and great health care coverage.

Amazon

Amazon is the company that changed the face of the book world and then moved on to conquer other markets, too. The release of their e-book reader Kindle revolutionized digital books and Amazon Prime is moving in on the digital entertainment scene. Today, Amazon sells just about everything and has a finger in just about every slice of modern life—and its corporate campus is located right in downtown Seattle. Jobs available range from editing to order picking to software development to finance and sales. Benefits include stock options, health care and dental coverage, stock options, and time off.

Paccar

Paccar doesn’t receive nearly as much press as other large companies in Seattle, but you likely see their products every day. Paccar builds, designs, and provides support for light- to heavy-weight trucks under the names Kenworth, Peterbilt and DAF. Paccar has a number of divisions located in the Northwest, including its financial, IT, leasing, parts, and tech center. Jobs vary across fields as diverse as financial, HR, IT, production and sales. Their benefits package includes health and dental insurance, tuition reimbursement, stock options, and even a fully vested pension plan for employees who stay longer than five years.

Starbucks

Starbucks is ubiquitous in Seattle, Tacoma, and other Puget Sound cities—sometimes there is literally a Starbucks on every corner. And while this chain is known for its baristas in green and lineup of caffeinated beverages, there is much more to a career at this coffee giant than serving up a mocha. Careers can be on the retail frontline, or in any number of back office positions from finance to roasting plant jobs. Benefits include full insurance options, adoption assistance, bonuses, stock options, and a free pound of coffee every week!

Nordstrom

Nordstrom is a clothing retail leader known for top-notch customer service and fantastic shoes! Retail careers are available at the stores located in or near malls throughout the Puget Sound region, and support positions are available at the Seattle headquarters. These positions can be located at Nordstrom’s distribution center, in marketing or PR, merchandising, IT, or any other number of fields. Benefits include health and dental plans, retirement and stock options, and more.

Expeditors International of WA

Expeditors International of Washington is a logistics company that employs more than 13,000 people worldwide. The company works with transportation of all sorts, providing solutions in air and ocean freight consolidation, customs clearance, and more. Expeditors is an excellent company for careers in accounting, insurance, and sales professionals, but also offers a number of unique fields in distribution, customs clearance, ocean freight and security.

Weyerhaeuser

Weyerhaeuser is a leader in the world of wood and paper product innovation. In the Seattle area, they’re known for rows and rows of sustainable forests planted in Washington and you might spot their name on anything from your office paper to packing supplies. Career paths at Weyerhaeuser include accounting and finance, HR, IT, manufacturing and operations, sales, as well as in harder sciences. Benefits include full insurance options, a pension plan, as well as programs designed to improve the lives of the company’s employees from help quitting smoking to onsite health screenings.

Alaska

Alaska Airlines is the nation's fifth largest airline and is headquartered in SeaTac, Washington, just south of Seattle. With flights that go throughout the continental US, Alaska and Hawaii, the airline also has partnerships with Oneworld, SkyTeam, Star Alliance and others to expand its network. The airline offers a range of career paths, including everything from baggage handlers and customer service agents at SeaTac International Airport and other airports, to flight attendants and pilots, to corporate roles.

Expedia

Expedia Group is headquartered in Bellevue, just across Lake Washington from Seattle, and focuses on all things travel. The company owns and operates several major travel websites, including Expedia, Hotels, Hotwire, Travelocity and trivago. Career paths include IT jobs maintaining and building the websites, content creator jobs, as well as sales, marketing, management, customer service, finance, legal and more.
